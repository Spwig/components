Setting up Zettle (PayPal) Terminal

  1. Get a Zettle account — Sign up at zettle.com (or through PayPal for US merchants)
  2. Register as a developer — Go to the Zettle Developer Portal and create an application.
  3. Get your Client ID — In the Developer Portal, find your application's Client ID.
  4. Create an API key — Go to my.zettle.com, navigate to API integrations, and create an API key (JWT assertion) for your application.
  5. Get a PayPal Reader — Order a PayPal Reader from the Zettle shop. Note: the Reader Connect API requires the WiFi-connected PayPal Reader, not the Bluetooth-only Zettle Reader 2.
  6. Connect your reader to WiFi — Power on the reader and visit http://192.168.4.1/ in a browser on a device connected to the reader's temporary WiFi to configure your network.
  7. Link the reader — The reader displays an 8-character pairing code. Enter this in Spwig POS to link the reader to your account.
  8. Test the connection — Use the "Test Connection" button in the POS Terminal Provider settings to verify connectivity.

Supported Devices

  • PayPal Reader — WiFi-connected card reader supporting chip & PIN, contactless (NFC), and magstripe. Accepts Visa, Mastercard, American Express, and digital wallets (Apple Pay, Google Pay).

Important Notes

  • The Zettle Reader 2 (Bluetooth-only) is not supported — it requires a native mobile SDK.
  • No sandbox environment is available. Testing uses real hardware with automatically reversed transactions.
  • Refunds for card-present payments must be initiated through the Zettle dashboard or app.
