PayPal Payment Setup

Why Choose PayPal? Accept payments from 400+ million active accounts worldwide. PayPal offers trusted checkout with buyer protection, supporting credit/debit cards, PayPal wallet, Venmo, and Buy Now Pay Later across 200+ markets and 25+ currencies.

Step 1: Create Your PayPal Business Account

  1. Visit PayPal Business Sign Up
  2. Select "Business Account" and click "Get Started"
  3. Enter your business email and create a password
  4. Complete the business profile with your company details
  5. Verify your email address
Tip: If you already have a personal PayPal account, you can upgrade it to a Business account from your account settings.

Step 2: Get Your REST API Credentials

  1. Go to the PayPal Developer Dashboard
  2. Log in with your PayPal Business account credentials
  3. Navigate to Apps & Credentials
  4. Toggle between Sandbox and Live mode as needed
  5. Click "Create App" to create a new REST API application
  6. Enter an app name (e.g., "My Store") and click "Create App"
  7. You will see your Client ID displayed on the app details page
  8. Click "Show" under Secret to reveal your Client Secret
  9. Important: Copy both the Client ID and Secret immediately
Security: Never share your API credentials. Keep your Client Secret secure and do not commit it to version control or expose it in client-side code.

Step 3: Configure Webhooks (Recommended)

  1. In the Developer Dashboard, scroll down to Webhooks
  2. Click "Add Webhook"
  3. Enter your webhook URL:
  4. Select the following events:
    • PAYMENT.CAPTURE.COMPLETED
    • PAYMENT.CAPTURE.DENIED
    • PAYMENT.CAPTURE.REFUNDED
    • CHECKOUT.ORDER.APPROVED
    • CHECKOUT.ORDER.COMPLETED
  5. Click "Save"
  6. Copy the Webhook ID displayed after saving (you will need this for signature verification)
Why Webhooks? Webhooks provide real-time notifications when payments are completed, denied, or refunded. This ensures your orders are updated immediately without relying on customer redirect.

Step 4: Enter Your Credentials Below

  1. Paste your Client ID from the Developer Dashboard
  2. Paste your Client Secret
  3. If you configured webhooks, paste the Webhook ID
  4. Choose your Environment:
    • Sandbox: For testing with simulated payments (no real money)
    • Live: For processing real transactions
  5. Click "Test Connection" to verify your credentials

Step 5: Test with Sandbox Accounts

Testing Mode: PayPal provides pre-configured sandbox buyer and seller accounts for testing. No real money is used in sandbox mode.

Sandbox Test Accounts:

  1. Go to Developer Dashboard - Sandbox Accounts
  2. You will find pre-created Business and Personal sandbox accounts
  3. Use the Personal account email and password to simulate buyer payments
  4. Default sandbox password is typically shown when you click the account details

Test Credit Cards:

  • Visa: 4032 0350 0516 6450
  • Mastercard: 5425 2334 3010 9903
  • Amex: 3714 496353 98431

Use any future expiration date and any 3-digit CVV (4 digits for Amex).

Step 6: Go Live

  1. Ensure your PayPal Business account is fully verified
  2. Test thoroughly in Sandbox mode first
  3. In the Developer Dashboard, switch to Live mode
  4. Create a new Live REST API app (or use the same app in live mode)
  5. Copy the Live Client ID and Live Client Secret
  6. Update your credentials here and switch Environment to Live
  7. Set up a new webhook with your Live app for production events
  8. Process a small test transaction to verify everything works
