This guide will help you set up Twilio to send WhatsApp messages from your store. WhatsApp Business API allows you to reach customers on their preferred messaging platform.

Time Required: 10-15 minutes
Prerequisites: Twilio account (free trial or paid)

Important: WhatsApp Business Policies

WhatsApp Messaging Rules:
  • 24-Hour Window: Free-form messages can only be sent within 24 hours of the customer's last message to you
  • Template Messages: Outside the 24-hour window, you must use pre-approved templates
  • Opt-in Required: Customers must explicitly opt-in to receive WhatsApp messages
  • Business Content: Messages must comply with WhatsApp's commerce and business policies

Setup Options

Twilio offers two ways to get started with WhatsApp:

Option A: Sandbox (Testing) - Quick setup for testing, limited features
Option B: Production (Recommended) - Full WhatsApp Business API access, requires Meta approval

Option A: WhatsApp Sandbox (Quick Start)

1

Access the Sandbox

  1. Log in to your Twilio Console
  2. Navigate to Messaging → Try it out → Send a WhatsApp message
  3. Or go directly to: WhatsApp Sandbox
2

Connect Your Phone

  1. You'll see a sandbox number (e.g., +14155238886)
  2. Send a WhatsApp message to this number with the code shown (e.g., "join <keyword>")
  3. You should receive a confirmation message
3

Configure the Provider

Field Value
Account SID Your Twilio Account SID (starts with AC)
Auth Token Your Twilio Auth Token
WhatsApp Number The sandbox number (e.g., +14155238886)
Sandbox Limitations:
  • Can only message phones that have "joined" the sandbox
  • Uses a shared Twilio number, not your business number
  • No access to message templates
  • For testing only, not production use

Option B: Production WhatsApp Setup

1

Request Access

  1. Log in to your Twilio Console
  2. Go to Messaging → Senders → WhatsApp senders
  3. Click Register WhatsApp Sender
  4. Follow the guided setup process
2

Set Up Meta Business Account

  1. Create or connect your Meta Business Account
  2. Verify your business with Meta
  3. Complete the WhatsApp Business API request
  4. Wait for approval (typically 1-3 business days)
3

Register Your Number

  1. Once approved, purchase a Twilio phone number
  2. Or use your existing business phone number
  3. Complete the WhatsApp sender registration in Twilio
  4. Verify your number with the OTP sent via SMS
4

Create Message Templates

  1. In Twilio Console, go to Messaging → Content Editor
  2. Create templates for transactional messages (order confirmations, receipts, etc.)
  3. Submit templates for approval
  4. Wait for Meta to approve your templates (24-48 hours)
Tip: Start with simple templates. Templates with excessive marketing language may be rejected.
5

Configure the Provider

Field Value
Account SID Your Twilio Account SID (starts with AC)
Auth Token Your Twilio Auth Token
WhatsApp Number Your registered WhatsApp number (e.g., +15551234567)

WhatsApp Pricing

WhatsApp uses conversation-based pricing, not per-message pricing:

  • User-initiated (Service): When a customer messages you first
  • Business-initiated (Marketing/Utility): When you message first with templates

Pricing varies by country. Example rates per conversation:

Region User-Initiated Business-Initiated
North America ~$0.0088 ~$0.0147
UK/Western Europe ~$0.0516 ~$0.0858

See Twilio WhatsApp Pricing for current rates.

Troubleshooting

Error: "Number not registered for WhatsApp"

  • Ensure you've completed WhatsApp sender registration
  • Verify the phone number format is correct (E.164 with +)
  • Check that your Meta Business account is approved

Error: "Template not approved"

  • Wait for template approval (24-48 hours)
  • Check template status in Twilio Console
  • Revise and resubmit if rejected

Error: "Outside 24-hour window"

  • You can only send free-form messages within 24 hours of customer's last message
  • Use approved templates for business-initiated conversations

Messages Not Delivered

  • Check Message Logs in Twilio Console
  • Verify recipient has WhatsApp installed
  • Ensure recipient hasn't blocked your number

Best Practices

  • Get explicit opt-in: Always obtain consent before sending WhatsApp messages
  • Respond quickly: Take advantage of the 24-hour window for free-form messaging
  • Use templates wisely: Create templates for common transactional messages
  • Respect preferences: Provide easy opt-out options
  • Keep it professional: WhatsApp messages should be valuable to the customer
